‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

What topics are covered?

We cover a wide range of economics topics including:

  • Microeconomics: Supply and demand, market structures, consumer behavior, production theory
  • Macroeconomics: GDP, inflation, unemployment, fiscal and monetary policy
  • International Economics: Trade, exchange rates, globalization
  • Development Economics: Economic growth, poverty, inequality
  • Behavioral Economics: Psychology and decision-making

How often is new content added?

New articles and notes are added regularly, typically several times per month. You can check the "Recent Notes" section on the homepage or the "All Notes" page to see the latest additions.

Can I use these notes for my studies?

Absolutely! These notes are designed to be educational resources. Feel free to use them for personal learning, study, and reference. However, please cite appropriately if you use them in academic work.

How do I navigate the site?

There are several ways to find content:

  • Search: Use the search bar to find articles by keywords
  • Browse by Category: Visit the Categories page to explore topics
  • View All Notes: See all articles on the All Notes page
  • Featured Content: Check the homepage for highlighted articles

Are there prerequisites for understanding the content?

Most articles are written to be accessible to readers with a basic understanding of economics. More advanced topics will include explanations of prerequisite concepts or links to foundational articles.

Can I suggest topics or corrections?

Yes! I welcome suggestions for new topics and corrections to existing content. Please use the contact information above to reach out with your ideas or feedback.

Is the content peer-reviewed?

While these notes are carefully researched and based on established economic theory, they are personal study notes and not formally peer-reviewed. Always cross-reference with textbooks and academic sources for critical research or academic work.
